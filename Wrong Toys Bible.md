# Wrong Toys Bible v1.1

## Vision
Bring childhood imagination to life through tiny living worlds.

## Core Identity
Wrong Toys is not a toy showcase or AI showcase. It is a universe where toys are alive and everyday childhood imagination becomes reality.

## Relationship to Wrong Mecha
- Wrong Mecha: premium cinematic canon series.
- Wrong Toys: daily experimental Shorts for growth.

## Target Audience
Adults (18–40) driven by nostalgia, curiosity and satisfying miniature worlds.

## Core Rules
1. Toys are alive.
2. The world feels like a real miniature diorama.
3. Macro photography aesthetic over obvious AI look.
4. One simple conflict per episode.
5. Visual storytelling first.
6. Creatures are citizens, not automatically villains.
7. No random destruction without purpose.
8. Childhood imagination first.

## Production Philosophy
Hook First. Story Second. Production Third.

## Production Workflow
1. Find the Hero Frame.
2. Pass the Mute Test (works without audio).
3. Build one Curiosity Question.
4. Create story around the hook.
5. Generate Hero Image Reference.
6. Generate Scene 1 using Image Reference.
7. Continue remaining scenes using Start Frame.
8. Add SFX, music and short dialogue.

## Hook Rules
- Spend the most time designing the first frame.
- First frame must stop scrolling in under one second.
- Every hook should create one clear question.
- Story exists to answer that question.

## Audio Rules
- Audio enhances the video but never carries the hook.
- Videos must still work when muted.
- Dialogue should be very short (1–3 words or one short sentence).

## DNA
A tiny living world where childhood imagination becomes reality.
